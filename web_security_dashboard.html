Company Story
NovaPay was founded in 2021 by three fintech veterans who saw a gap in the market for a developer-first digital payments platform built for small and mid-size businesses. Frustrated by the complexity and cost of legacy payment processors, they set out to build a modern, API-native alternative that could handle domestic and cross-border money transfers at a fraction of the typical cost.
After closing a $4.2M seed round in 2022, NovaPay launched its core payments API and quickly onboarded 120+ business customers across e-commerce, SaaS, and logistics. In late 2024, the company raised a $18M Series A to expand its engineering team, pursue PCI-DSS Level 1 certification, and begin SOC 2 Type II audit preparation.
Today, NovaPay processes over $2.3M in daily transaction volume across 14 countries, with a 50-person team split between engineering, product, operations, and go-to-market. The company operates entirely on AWS (us-east-1 primary, eu-west-1 failover) and maintains a remote-first culture with a small Austin office for leadership and operations staff.
Compliance Obligations
🔒 PCI-DSS Level 1 ✓ SOC 2 Type II (in progress)
As a payment processor handling cardholder data, NovaPay is subject to PCI-DSS Level 1 — the most stringent tier, requiring an annual on-site assessment by a Qualified Security Assessor (QSA). The company is also completing its first SOC 2 Type II audit to satisfy enterprise customer requirements around security, availability, and confidentiality.
Threat Landscape
💳
Payment Fraud & Account Takeover
NovaPay's transaction APIs are a prime target for fraudsters using stolen credentials or synthetic identities. Account takeover via credential stuffing poses direct financial risk to customers and reputational damage to NovaPay.
🎣
Phishing & Social Engineering
With a remote-first workforce of 50 employees, spear-phishing attacks targeting finance and engineering staff are a constant threat. A single compromised credential can expose access to the AWS production environment.
🔓
API Abuse & Injection Attacks
NovaPay's payment platform exposes dozens of REST API endpoints. Without comprehensive WAF and input validation, attackers may exploit injection vulnerabilities or abuse rate limits to enumerate accounts or manipulate transactions.
🏢
Insider Threat
Employees and contractors with access to payment data, customer PII, and AWS infrastructure represent an insider risk — whether malicious or accidental. Privileged access abuse is particularly dangerous given the financial data NovaPay holds.
🔗
Third-Party & Supply Chain Risk
NovaPay depends on multiple third-party vendors for banking rails, KYC verification, fraud scoring, and cloud infrastructure. A breach at any of these partners could directly compromise NovaPay's data or service availability.

Current Posture

NovaPay has established a solid foundation in core Preventive and Directive controls. Multi-Factor Authentication, encryption at rest via AWS KMS, a formal Information Security Policy, and a documented Incident Response Plan are all fully implemented. UEBA and Fraud/Transaction Monitoring are also operational, reflecting appropriate investment in the company's highest-value risk area — payment integrity.

However, significant gaps remain in the company's ability to detect threats in real time and respond operationally. Detective controls are underrepresented relative to the threat landscape, and two high-priority Operational controls — Manual Transaction Review and Incident Response Tabletop Exercises — are entirely missing. The Physical category is thin, and no Privileged Access Management solution is in place for AWS admin accounts.

Critical Findings

CRITICALNo Manual Transaction Review process exists. NovaPay's sole Compensating control is Missing. Given known false-negative rates in automated fraud detection, the absence of a human review fallback creates direct PCI-DSS Requirement 10 exposure.

CRITICALIncident Response Plan has never been exercised. A documented plan without rehearsal is insufficient. Industry data consistently shows organizations without practiced IR plans take significantly longer to contain breaches.

CRITICALNo Privileged Access Management (PAM). AWS admin credentials without session recording, approval workflows, or just-in-time access represent an unchecked insider threat and a direct SOC 2 gap.

HIGHPartial coverage across EDR, SIEM, WAF, and RBAC. Four core Technical controls remain partially deployed, leaving visibility and access control gaps across the environment.

Recommendations

The following actions are recommended in order of urgency: (1) establish the Manual Transaction Review process within 30 days; (2) deploy a PAM solution for AWS admin accounts within 45 days; (3) schedule and conduct the first IR Tabletop Exercise within 60 days; (4) complete full EDR rollout within 45 days; (5) implement a Secure Device Disposal procedure before the next hardware refresh; and (6) develop a Deterrent control layer including login banners and a visitor escort policy.

Conclusion

NovaPay's security posture is functional but maturing. The company has made sound investments in preventive and governance controls appropriate for a Series A fintech. Addressing the six priority actions above would meaningfully reduce breach risk, close PCI-DSS Level 1 compliance gaps, and position the company favorably for its upcoming SOC 2 Type II audit.
